In-Hand Salary Calculator: CTC to Take-Home Breakdown

Apr 2026 · 7 min read · By Calc Labz Team

Why Your CTC and In-Hand Salary Are So Different

If your offer letter says ₹12 LPA but your bank account receives ₹75,000/month, you’re not being cheated — you’re seeing the gap between CTC (Cost to Company) and in-hand salary. CTC includes every rupee your employer spends on you: base salary, HRA, PF contributions, gratuity provision, insurance premiums, and sometimes even meal coupons and cab allowances. Your in-hand salary is what’s left after mandatory deductions.

CTC Components Explained

ComponentTypical % of CTCTaxable?
Basic Salary40–50%Yes, fully
HRA (House Rent Allowance)20–25%Partially exempt if you pay rent
Special Allowance15–25%Yes, fully
Employer PF (12% of Basic)~5–6%No (not in your hand)
Gratuity Provision~4.8%No (paid on exit after 5 years)
Insurance / Perks1–3%Varies

Worked Example: ₹12 LPA CTC Breakdown

Let’s trace how ₹12,00,000 CTC becomes your monthly in-hand amount:

Basic Salary (40%)4,80,00040,000
HRA (50% of Basic, metro)2,40,00020,000
Special Allowance2,42,40020,200
Employer PF (12% of Basic)57,6004,800
Gratuity (4.81% of Basic)23,0881,924
Medical Insurance12,0001,000
Gross Salary (excl. PF, gratuity, insurance)9,62,40080,200

Deductions from gross salary:

  • Employee PF (12% of Basic): ₹4,800/month
  • Professional Tax: ₹200/month (in most states)
  • Income Tax TDS: ~₹4,200/month (new regime, after rebate adjustments)

Net in-hand salary: ~₹71,000/month from a ₹12 LPA CTC. That’s about 71% of CTC reaching your bank account.

What Eats Into Your Take-Home?

The biggest deductions for most salaried employees:

  • EPF contribution: 12% of basic from your side, 12% from employer (total 24% of basic locked until retirement). Use the EPF calculator to see your corpus projection.
  • Income tax TDS: Varies by regime and deductions. Use the income tax calculator to estimate your exact TDS.
  • Professional tax: ₹200/month in most states (max ₹2,500/year). See our professional tax calculator for state-wise rates.

Old Regime vs New Regime Impact on In-Hand Salary

Your choice of tax regime directly affects monthly TDS and therefore your in-hand salary. At ₹12 LPA with no deductions, the new regime gives you zero tax (thanks to ₹75K standard deduction + Section 87A rebate up to ₹12L taxable income). With the old regime, you’d need at least ₹3.75L in deductions (80C + 80D + HRA) to match that. For most people below ₹15 LPA, the new regime results in higher take-home pay.

Common Mistakes When Evaluating a Job Offer

  • Comparing CTC directly: A ₹14 LPA CTC with 50% variable pay might give you less monthly cash than a ₹12 LPA fixed CTC.
  • Ignoring the PF component: Higher basic salary means more PF deduction now, but it’s your retirement savings. Don’t see it as lost money.
  • Forgetting reimbursements: Meal cards, LTA, and phone reimbursements are tax-free but need to be claimed — they don’t come automatically.
  • Not negotiating structure: You can sometimes negotiate a lower basic (reducing PF lock-in) for higher special allowance, increasing immediate take-home.
