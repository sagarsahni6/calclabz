How Speed & Distance Calculator is Calculated

This calculator uses the fundamental kinematic equation: Distance = Speed × Time (d = s × t). Rearranged forms: Speed = Distance / Time, Time = Distance / Speed. Unit conversions use standard SI conversion factors. Results account for consistent unit handling across km/h, m/s, mph, and other speed units.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate distance from speed and time?

Distance = Speed × Time. At 60 km/h for 2.5 hours: 60 × 2.5 = 150 km. Keep units consistent — if speed is in km/h, time must be in hours (not minutes). 30 minutes = 0.5 hours.

How do I find average speed for a trip?

Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time. A 300 km trip taking 5 hours (including stops): 300/5 = 60 km/h average. If you drive 150 km at 80 km/h and 150 km at 60 km/h, average is NOT 70 — it is 68.6 km/h (harmonic mean).
